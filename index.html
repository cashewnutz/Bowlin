Electrical Muscle Stimulation (EMS) is a training method that delivers electrical impulses that stimulate muscle contractions. EMS amplifies the natural process of muscle stimulation to encourage your muscles to work harder. Depending on the pulse frequency, duration of contraction, duration of rest, and length of training session, we can simulate various types of muscle “work”.

At Electro Flex Fitness, our trained personal trainers lead each private (or semi-private) session. After reviewing all contraindications, we begin with a body scan that reports a series of biometrics, from body fat percentage to resting metabolic rate. It gives you and your trainer a comprehensive picture of your current state to monitor future progress and helps set end goal. The trainer fits you in a Electro Flex Fitness-issued workout clothing and a suit which is fitted with carbon-fiber electrodes. The trainer mists some water on your suit’s electrodes to make the signal more conducive and more effective. The suit is then coupled with the battery-operated EMS machine. The trainer monitors and adjusts the electrical pulses to your level of comfort for each muscle group. Once the levels are set, then the trainer will lead you through 20 minutes of workout which includes series of basic movements, without using any weights. Throughout the 20-minute workout, the trainer may adjust the electrical pulses amplification up or down, based on your level of comfort and threshold. Your actual workout will be low-impact and deceptively easy. As the electrical impulses continue, you will feel a tingling sensation across your muscle groups; you will also feel yourcontracting at the same time. The trainer will direct you to time your breathing to each impulse (muscle contraction), not lock your knees, and squeeze the muscle group you want to focus on during the exercise. The trainer will also challenge you to take on more resistance. It will feel like you’re doing so much more than simply squatting, lunging, and reaching overhead. Within 20 minutes, we will have stimulated same number of muscles as a typical 60-90 minutes of conventional workout.

Most clients see results after 1 sessions a week and after 4 to 6 weeks. With a 20-minute training duration, Electro Flex EMS training can stimulate the same muscles as 60-90 minutes of conventional training, that is dependent on smaller muscle group training, using a sequence of exercises. Our clients feel a difference after their first EMS session. Below are the typical responses from our clients: 24–48 HOURS: After a day or two, you’ll feel some muscle stiffness, which is normal and is indicative of your body’s recovery process. 4 WEEKS: Within a period of about 4 weeks, you might feel stronger as you improve your muscle mass. 1–2 MONTHS: After roughly 6 to 8 sessions (6 to 8 weeks) you may not only feel, but also see a visible difference from the EMS training in the form of gradually increased muscle formation and body composition change (depending on your diet). EMS training should be done regularly to ensure and maintain optimal results.

You can do EMS every 3-4 days (twice a week) due to the high frequency of the impulses. We recommend taking 24 hours of rest post session.

EMS has been scientifically proven to be effective for people of different fitness levels and different age groups. An athlete can train next to a physically inactive client and both will receive challenging workouts that fit their level of exercise. Similarly, a younger adult and an older adult can train next to each other and training can be geared with not only intensity-level but also with impact- level. Just like conventional exercises, our EMS training also has certain contraindications. We require our clients be over the age of 18 (14+ with Parental Consent). Also, our EMS training is not advisable for certain groups who should not be exposed to physical stress or engage in sports; please refer to the consent forms for the full list. Also, pregnant clients are not able to train with EMS. We always recommend speaking with your General Practitioner if you are unsure.
